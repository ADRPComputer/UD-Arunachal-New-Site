Introduction

The Department of Urban Development & Housing, Arunachal Pradesh was created on 04.04.1995 vide order OM-1/95(pt) to function as Nodal Department for formulating policies of Urban Development activities, creation of urban infrastructure, implementation & monitoring of various poverty alleviation programme. Today, the Department is headed by a Chief Engineer-cum-Director with functional 23 (Twenty Three) divisions throughout the state. The state Govt. has declared the Urban Development Department as Works Department.

Main Functions

  • To implement Urban Poverty Alleviation Programmes.
  • To provide basic ammenities, services and property rights to slum dwellers.
  • To construct cluster housing schemes for Below Poverty Line (BPL) familes and Safai Karmacharies.
  • To create infrastructure in all 30 (Thirty) urban centres.
  • To maintain Urban Sanitation and Solid Waste Management.
  • To implement drainage and sewarage system in Urban Areas.
  • To clear Public streets, places, sewers etc.
  • To allot and manage Government estates in the Capital Town.
  • To maintain housing stock.
  • To control and regulate real estates development.
  • To control and monitor all activities of State Urban Development Agency (SUDA) and District Urban Development Agencies (DUDAs).
  • Matter relating to information and elections of Municipal Boards, Municipal Committee, Town Area Committee and other forms of Municipalities for town and urban areas.
